  • Reduce food waste at source – enabling food businesses to donate rather than discard.

📉 The Problem: The Cost of Wasting Food in India

India wastes 68 million tons of food annually, while 40 crore people struggle with food insecurity.

₹92,000 crores worth of food is discarded every year—this could feed 100 million people.

  • 25% of India's freshwater is used to grow food that is never eaten.
  • 300 million barrels of oil go into producing wasted food.
  • Landfills filled with food waste generate 3.3 billion tons of CO₂ annually, accelerating climate change.

Many restaurants and event organizers discard surplus food due to a lack of reliable redistribution channels.

Religious and cultural events also generate thousands of uneaten meals daily, with no structured mechanism to distribute them.

💡 AnnSetu’s Solution: "Rescue, Reallocate, Reuse"

Our AI-powered system connects food donors with NGOs and food banks in real time.

Key features include:

  • Automated tracking to monitor donations, tax benefits, and environmental impact.
  • Logistics optimization to ensure fresh food reaches recipients quickly.
  • Corporate and government partnerships to encourage CSR participation and policy advocacy.
  • Transparency through impact reports for businesses, food tracking for NGOs, and real-time analytics for the government.

AnnSetu creates a seamless bridge between those who have surplus food and those who need it most.
